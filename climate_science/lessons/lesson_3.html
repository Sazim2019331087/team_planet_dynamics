Global warming refers to the long-term rise in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels. These activities increase the concentration of greenhouse gases, trapping more heat and accelerating the warming of the planet.
üî• How are heatwaves connected to global warming?
Due to global warming, heatwaves are becoming more frequent and intense. As average temperatures rise, extreme weather events like heatwaves occur more often and with greater severity, making them a clear indicator of climate change.
üöÄ What role does NASA play in tracking global warming?
NASA uses Earth-observing satellites to monitor land, ocean, and atmospheric temperatures. These missions, like Aqua and Terra, help track how much the Earth's temperature has risen and provide critical data for understanding climate trends.

Rising temperatures affect ecosystems, agriculture, and human health. Heatwaves can strain resources, increase wildfire risks, and cause health issues. If we don't reduce greenhouse gas emissions, heatwaves will only get worse.

Global warming is driving more frequent and intense heatwaves, reminding us that urgent climate action is needed.
